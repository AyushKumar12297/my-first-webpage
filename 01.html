Kashi Vishwanath Temple

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is a sacred Hindu temple located in Varanasi, Uttar Pradesh, India. It is one of the most revered temples in India and is dedicated to Lord Shiva.

The temple is situated on the western bank of the Ganges River and is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, or sacred shrines, of Lord Shiva.

History

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple has a rich history dating back to the 11th century. It was built by the ruler of the Gahadavala dynasty, but was destroyed by the Mughal emperor Aurangzeb in the 17th century.

The temple was rebuilt in the 18th century by the Maratha ruler, Rani Ahilya Bai Holkar.

Architecture

The Kashi Vishwanath Temple is a stunning example of Hindu architecture. The temple complex consists of several smaller shrines and a large central shrine, which houses the sacred linga of Lord Shiva.

The temple is built using white marble and is adorned with intricate carvings and sculptures.
